## Model Details

### 1. Flat Pricing

**Structure**: Fixed monthly fee per tier

| Tier | Monthly Fee |
|------|-------------|
| Starter | $29 |
| Pro | $99 |
| Enterprise | $299 |

**Pros**: Simple, predictable revenue  
**Cons**: 10 unprofitable customers, no usage alignment

### 2. Tiered Pricing

**Structure**: Base fee + included calls + overage charges

| Tier | Base Fee | Included Calls | Overage Rate |
|------|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Starter | $29 | 100 | $0.15/call |
| Pro | $99 | 500 | $0.12/call |
| Enterprise | $299 | 2,000 | $0.10/call |

**Pros**: Fair usage-based component, protects against heavy users  
**Cons**: More complex billing, potential customer friction on overages

### 3. Pure Usage-Based

**Structure**: Pay per use only (2.5x cost markup)

**Pros**: Perfect cost alignment, no unprofitable customers  
**Cons**: Unpredictable revenue, may deter light users, highest total cost to customers

### 4. Hybrid Pricing

**Structure**: Reduced base fee + cost-plus pricing

| Tier | Base Fee | Cost Multiplier |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Starter | $19 | 2.0x (100% markup) |
| Pro | $49 | 1.8x (80% markup) |
| Enterprise | $149 | 1.5x (50% markup) |

**Pros**: Balanced approach, predictable base + fair usage component  
**Cons**: Requires cost transparency, moderate complexity
